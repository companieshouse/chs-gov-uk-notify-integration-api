Dear [[${psc_name}]]

On [[${date}]], [[${company_name}]] notified us that you are a person with significant control (PSC) of the company. A PSC is someone who has some ownership or control of the company, for example because they have more than 25% of shares or voting rights.

Under the power provided by section 790LM of the Companies Act 2006, the Registrar of Companies now directs you to deliver a statement confirming your identity is verified, and to do so within 14 days of the date of this direction letter.

Instructions on how to meet these identity verification requirements are on the back of this letter.

You must comply with identity verification requirements by [[${extension_date}]].

If you do not comply, you may be committing an offence. You may face
prosecution and a fine through the courts, or a financial penalty. In the future,
we’ll publish a note against your name on the Companies House public register.

If you are not [[${psc_name}]], you must forward this letter to them immediately.

There are 2 things you must do to comply with identity verification requirements:

1. Verify your identity

For information on how to do this, search online for ‘Verifying your identity for Companies House’, or visit: gov.uk/verifying-your-identity-for-companieshouse

After you’ve verified your identity, you'll get a Companies House personal code. If you already have a personal code, you do not need to verify your identity again.

2. Provide your Companies House personal code

You must provide your personal code so that we can connect your verified identity to the correct PSC name on our records.

To do this, search online for the ‘Provide verification details for a PSC’ service, or visit: gov.uk/provide-verificationdetails-for-a-psc

If you are unable to complete both steps by the deadline

Before you reach your deadline, use the ‘Provide verification details for a PSC’ service to request a 14 day extension:
gov.uk/provide-verificationdetails-for-a-psc

Why PSCs need to verify

This new legal requirement is a result of the Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act. All company directors and PSCs must verify their identity to prove they are who
they say they are. This has been introduced to help prevent people using companies for illegal purposes.
