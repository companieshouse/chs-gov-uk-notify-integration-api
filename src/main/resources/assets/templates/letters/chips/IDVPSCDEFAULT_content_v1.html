Dear [[${psc_name}]],

You have not delivered a statement to Companies House to confirm your identity.

This is a new legal requirement under the Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023. All company directors and PSCs must verify their identity and submit an identity verification statement to Companies House to prove they are who they say they are. This has been introduced to increase transparency and help prevent people using companies for illegal purposes.

You must verify your identity and submit a statement confirming that you've done so.

If you are unable to comply on time due to exceptional circumstances, you must submit your reason in writing by [[${action_due_date}]].

Instructions on how to do this are on the back of this letter.


If you do not comply, you may be committing an offence. You may face
prosecution and a fine through the courts, or a financial penalty. In the future,
we’ll publish a note against your name on the Companies House public register.

There are 2 things you must do to comply with identity verification requirements

1. Verify your identity

For information on how to do this, search online for ‘Verifying your identity for Companies House’, or visit: gov.uk/guidance/verifying-your-identity-for-companies-house

After you’ve verified your identity, you’ll get a unique identifier, called a Companies House personal code.

If you already have a personal code, you do not need to verify your identity again.

2. Provide your Companies House personal code

This is so we can connect your verified identity to the PSC name on our records.

Search online for the ‘Provide identity verification details for a person with significant control’ service, or visit:
gov.uk/provide-identity-verification-details-for-a-psc

You’ll also be asked to tick a statement confirming that you’ve verified your identity.

You need to do this even if you’ve already done it for other roles you have at this company, or at other companies.
