There are 2 things you must do to comply with identity verification requirements.

1. Verify your identity

For information on how to do this, search online for ‘Verifying your identity for Companies House’, or visit: gov.uk/guidance/verifying-your-identity-for-companies-house

After you’ve verified your identity, you'll get a Companies House personal code.

If you already have a personal code, you do not need to verify your identity again.

2. Provide your Companies House personal code

This is so we can connect your verified identity to the PSC name on our records.

Search online for the ‘Provide identity verification details for a person with significant control’ service, or visit: gov.uk/provide-identity-verification-details-for-a-psc

You’ll also be asked to tick a statement confirming that you’ve verified your identity.

You need to do this even if you’ve already done it for other roles you have at this company, or at other companies.

If you are unable to complete both steps by the deadline

gov.uk/provide-identity-verification-details-for-a-psc

Why PSCs need to verify their identity

This is a new legal requirement under the Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023.

All company directors and PSCs must verify their identity to prove they are who they say they are.

This has been introduced to help prevent people using companies for illegal purposes.
